3. Reflect on how confident you feel using each of these problem-solving techniques/processes:

  • Pseudocode - I'm working on how I write my thoughts. I'm still not quite writing the write prompts to formulate a logical answer but it's improving.
  • Trying something - I felt more confident just giving things a go. Using console.log to understand what the current output is and using that to help my code.
  • Rubber ducky method - This one is still a bit strange to me. I like to just talk out loud to myself and that seems to help. Treating myself as the rubber ducky.
  • Reading error messages - Not great. But understanding a little more because I saw many of them.
  • Console.logging - Improving! I relied on this alot during this sprint.
  • Googling - Getting pretty good at this. I googled ALOT.
  • Asking your peers for help - Improving at this too, and trying to not feel like i'm bothering others with my questions.
  • Asking coaches for help - I found that I reached out for help, but then in the space between sending the message and hearing back, I found the answer.
  • Improving your process with reflection - I guess i'm being more honest with myself in thes reflections. Transparently saying what my experience has been.

4. Reflect on a time you were reluctant to ask for help. Consider what made you reluctant to do so. What might you try differently next time? Earlier this week I felt reluctant to ask for help, because I wanted the satisfaction of completing things on my own. I'm still not quite there yet, but I will be. Next time I will use the pomodoro timer more often and set myself a time limit of solving problems. If I havn't solved it in the time, I will reach out for help. That way I won't get stuck as much.
