Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision

I was an educator a top NZ tertiary institute for 4 years. I decided to leave this role at the end of last year as my values as an educator were not being met by the way the institute handled their curriculum, communication and treatment of staff. When I started this role, I told myself that I would not continue working here if I became bitter or sour to my job, as it’s important for me that students learning experience remain untouched by the inner workings of a university. The students learning became compromised, and over a long period of time I noticed nothing was changing. So I made the ethical decision to leave this position (which was the best education job I could get in my field), for my own creative, professional and spiritual integrity.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

I’m deeply influenced by the arts scene in Wellington. I have been surrounded by passionate, generous and motivated musicians since moving to Wellington 14 years ago. This environment has created a habit in me to keep working towards my goals, because the music industry is brutal, it’s easy to “fall out” of the scene. In the same respect, it’s inspired me to follow my own creative voice so I could pursue my own identity. My belief is that whether you like it or not, your creative identity is a reflection of your personal identity.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

One of my biggest strengths is my perseverance. Once I begin a task, I must complete it at all costs, it’s almost like a compulsion. Which fortunately, means that I suit being in an academic environment. I also like to believe that I hold great empathy and fairness for others and am happy and even excited to assist others when/where possible.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

I get overwhelmed with lots of new information in short periods of time. This along with some sensory overload is a bit of a limitation.
