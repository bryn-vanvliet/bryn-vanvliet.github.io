Emotional Intelligence

Emotional Intelligence is a core personal value. Many of the trials and tribulations of life can navigated with greater ease by developing & practicing emotional intelligence.

Emotional Intelligence is our response to emotions that arise. Strong emotions such as; anger, grief, anxiety can be debilitating, but we have a choose on how we are going to cognitively respond to these emotions.

For example, when I feel the frustration arise whilst i'm trying to make this blog function well (a good example), realising that I have a choice in how I respond to this emotion makes me feel empowered and in control.

In Stephen R. Covey's book titled "The 7 Habits Of Highly Effective People" he discusses the idea of "reactive" & "pro-active" responses. Consider this situation, you're driving down the road and cyclist smashes into your car. They are extremely mad because they think it's your fault. They are yelling at you and calling you names. A "reactive" response to this situation would be to get angry back, yell, scream, possibly get into a fight. A "pro-active" response might be to let this person experience the feelings that are happening, wait for them to calm down. Then through logic, patience and understanding, figure out how to solve the problem.

A reactive response only adds fuel to the fire. Whislt, a pro-active response resolves the problem quicker and easier whislt conserving your personal energy.
