Neuroplasticity

Understanding essentially how your brain learns and retains information is a key component to learning, and especially learning something new. There is a proven method of learning that utilizes the A.G.E.S method. Attention - In order to learn you need to pay attention and to not have distractions. Generation - Relating the new information to something they have already learned. Creating a meaningful connection and generation of new ideas. Emotion - Assign an emotion to the learning. This is more likely to remember the content. Also makes it fun or extra challenging? Spacing - Spacing out the learning makes it more manageable. Perhaps what you learn and the intervals you do it in is what this means.

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit.

Removing distractions whilst studying, glad to say I have been working on this lately. Fortunately relating new information to pre-existing things I understand is a method I have been using to learn languages. For example, using my knowledge of Portuguese to help learn Spanish, as they are similar. A little unclear how emotion works into this, but I think making the learning fun and engaging is helpful. I could treat the tasks as games instead of this untangible thing. Spacing - I need to have more spacing in my learning, as I try to learn everything in a rush, and don’t retain it.

Growth Mindset

Growth mindset is essentially how you view yourself learning, your frame of mind, your attitude towards learning. Growth mindset is relevant because understanding what constitutes a growth mindset plays a big role in the development of new skills. Currently I am learning a whole new skill from scratch and am dealing with multitudes of information constantly, which is overwhelming. Understanding what mindset I’m in and moving towards adopting the growth mindset more consistently I think will yield more positive results.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

I noticed myself reflecting on what mindset I’m currently in. I noticed traits of a fixed mindset which was alarming to me, as I normally feel like I embrace challenges and am always striving to be better.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

By recognizing what constitutes a fixed mindset, and noticing when I fall into those behaviors.
